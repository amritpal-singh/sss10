STEEL SEISMIC SYSTEMS 10

Permissible Structural Steel Seismic-Force-Resisting Systems in accordance with NBC 2010 and CSA S16-09

INSTRUCTIONS AND COMMENTS

Input

Location and spectral accelerations: Select the 'Province' and 'Location' from the list boxes. The appropriate values of spectral accelerations, Sa(0.2) and Sa(1.0), obtained from Appendix C of NBC 2010 for the chosen location, will be used in SSS10. THE USER MUST VERIFY THE APPLICABILITY OF THE NBC APPENDIX-‐C VALUES WITH THE AUTHORITY HAVING JURISDICTION. If any of the NBC values do not apply, click in the box labeled "Check to enter user‐specified spectral accelerations".

Building Importance Category: Select the NBC Building Importance Category from the list box.

Site Class: From the list box, select the site class in accordance with NBC sentence 4.1.8.4. When Site Class F is selected the user must also enter the values of site coefficients Fa and Fv.

Building Height: Enter the building height in metres.

Then click 'VIEW PERMISSIBLE SFRS' to view output.

Output

Project information and seismic data: A summary of the input information and associated parameters is given, including the values of Sa(0.2) and Sa(1.0), Importance Factor, IE, and Site Coefficients, Fa and Fv . Also shown are the values of IEFaSa(0.2) and IEFvSa(1.0), two important parameters that serve as key triggers for height restrictions.

Permissible structural steel seismic-force-resisting systems (SFRS) for this building: Only the permissible SFRS are listed. For each SFRS, the values of the Force Modification Factors, Rd and Ro, and the maximum height limit, if applicable, are also shown.

Comments

1. Conventional Construction is not permitted for:

a) Post-disaster buildings
b) Assembly occupancy buildings taller than 15 m, where IEFaSa(0.2) ≥ 0.35 or IEFvSa(1.0) > 0.3, and
c) Buildings for occupancies other than Assembly that exceed the maximum height limit shown in the output.

2. For Concentrically BF with chevron bracing and Limited-ductility CBF with tension-only braces, CSA S16-09 permits a relaxation of ductile design requirements in exchange for a lower height limit.

3. For a specific application, some permissible SFRS may be more suitable than others when other code requirements, costs, building layout and other architectural features, construction speed and practicality, etc. are taken into account.
